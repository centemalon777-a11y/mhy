⚡ Work, Energy and Power

SS1 Physics – Comprehensive Notes

Work

Work is done when a force is applied on a body and the body is displaced in the direction of the force. It is a measure of energy transferred by a force.

W = F × d × cosθ
where F = force applied (N), d = displacement (m), θ = angle between force and displacement.

Unit of Work: Joule (J)

Conditions for Work to be Done

  • A force must be applied.
  • The body must be displaced.
  • There must be a component of the force in the direction of displacement.

Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work. It exists in different forms such as mechanical, thermal, chemical, nuclear, and electrical energy.

Types of Mechanical Energy

  • Kinetic Energy (KE): The energy a body possesses due to motion.
    KE = ½mv²
    where m = mass (kg), v = velocity (m/s).
  • Potential Energy (PE): The energy stored in a body due to its position or configuration.
    PE = mgh
    where m = mass (kg), g = 9.8 m/s², h = height (m).

Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change from one form to another, but the total energy of an isolated system remains constant.

Power

Power is the rate of doing work or the rate of energy transfer.

P = W / t
where W = work done (J), t = time taken (s).

Unit of Power: Watt (W)

Efficiency

Efficiency of a machine is the ratio of useful work output to the total work input, expressed as a percentage.

Efficiency = (Useful Output / Input) × 100%

Example Problem

A force of 50 N moves a body 4 m in the direction of the force in 10 seconds. (a) Work done? (b) Power developed?

Work Done: W = F × d = 50 × 4 = 200 J

Power: P = W / t = 200 / 10 = 20 W

→ Work done is 200 J and Power is 20 W.

Applications of Work, Energy, and Power

  • Designing engines and machines.
  • Construction and lifting systems.
  • Electricity generation.
  • Transportation systems.

Conclusion

Work, energy, and power are interrelated concepts in mechanics. They are fundamental to understanding how forces affect motion and how machines perform tasks efficiently.
