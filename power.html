⚡ Power

SS1 Physics – Comprehensive Notes

Introduction

Power is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. It measures how quickly energy is used or work is performed.

Definition

Power is defined as the work done per unit time.

Power (P) = Work Done (W) / Time (t)

Its unit in SI is the watt (W), where 1 watt = 1 joule per second.

Forms of Power

  • Mechanical Power: Work done by machines, engines, or moving bodies.
  • Electrical Power: Work done by electric current in a circuit.
  • Chemical Power: Energy released during chemical reactions (e.g., batteries, fuel).

Power in Mechanics

When a constant force F moves an object with velocity v:

P = F × v

Electrical Power

In an electrical circuit:

P = V × I
P = I²R
P = V² / R

Where V = voltage, I = current, and R = resistance.

Efficiency

Machines are not 100% efficient. The efficiency of a machine is:

Efficiency (%) = (Useful Power Output ÷ Total Power Input) × 100

Applications of Power

  • Determining the capacity of electrical appliances (e.g., bulbs, heaters, fans).
  • Comparing the performance of machines and engines.
  • Measuring energy consumption in homes and industries.

Example

Example: A motor does 600 J of work in 30 seconds. Find its power output.

P = W / t = 600 / 30 = 20 W

Conclusion

Power is a vital concept in physics that connects work, energy, and time. It helps in analyzing machines, engines, and energy systems both in mechanics and electricity.
