Introduction

Elasticity is the property of a body that allows it to regain its original shape and size after the deforming force has been removed.

Examples of Elastic Materials

  • Rubber bands
  • Springs
  • Metals (when the stress is small)

Stress and Strain

Stress: Force applied per unit area.

Stress = Force / Area

Strain: The ratio of change in length to the original length.

Strain = Extension / Original Length

Young‚Äôs Modulus (Y)

It is the ratio of stress to strain, within the elastic limit.

Y = Stress / Strain

Elastic Limit

The maximum stress a material can withstand without permanent deformation. Beyond this point, the material does not return to its original shape.

Applications of Elasticity

  • Design of springs, bridges, and tall buildings.
  • Manufacturing of car suspensions and mattresses.
  • Measuring forces using spring balances.

Conclusion

Elasticity explains how materials behave under stress and strain. It is essential in engineering, construction, and the design of everyday objects.
