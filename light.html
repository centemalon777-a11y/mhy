Introduction

Light is a form of energy that makes things visible to the eye. It travels in straight lines as waves and also exhibits particle-like properties. Light enables us to see objects and plays an important role in vision, communication, and technology.

Nature of Light

Light has a dual nature:

  • It behaves as a wave (showing interference, diffraction, and polarization).
  • It behaves as a particle (showing photoelectric effect and energy quantization).

Properties of Light

  • It travels in straight lines in a homogeneous medium.
  • It can be reflected, refracted, diffracted, or absorbed.
  • Its speed in a vacuum is constant at approximately 3 √ó 10‚Å∏ m/s.
  • It can exhibit both wave and particle characteristics.

Laws of Reflection

  • The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
  • The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal all lie in the same plane.

Dispersion of Light

When white light passes through a glass prism, it splits into its component colors (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet). This phenomenon is called dispersion.

Applications of Light

  • Vision (seeing objects)
  • Photography and cameras
  • Lasers for surgery and communication
  • Optical fibers for data transfer
