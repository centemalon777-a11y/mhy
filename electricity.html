⚡ Electricity

SS1 Physics – Comprehensive Notes

Introduction

Electricity is the flow of electric charges (usually electrons) through a conductor. It is one of the most important sources of energy in modern life.

Types of Electricity

  • Static Electricity – caused by accumulation of charges on a surface.
  • Current Electricity – flow of charges through a conductor.

Basic Quantities

  • Charge (Q) – measured in Coulombs (C).
  • Current (I) – rate of flow of charge.
    I = Q / t
  • Voltage (V) – work done per unit charge.
    V = W / Q
  • Resistance (R) – opposition to flow of current.
    V = IR (Ohm’s Law)

Electric Power and Energy

P = VI
E = Pt

Where P = power, V = voltage, I = current, E = energy, t = time.

Applications of Electricity

  • Household lighting and appliances.
  • Industrial machines and equipment.
  • Transportation (electric cars, trains).
  • Communication devices and electronics.

Example

Example: A bulb is rated 60 W, 240 V. Find the current it uses.

P = VI ⇒ I = P / V = 60 / 240 = 0.25 A

Conclusion

Electricity powers our world, and its study in physics provides the foundation for modern technology.
