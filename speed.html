Introduction

Speed and velocity are fundamental concepts in physics that describe how fast an object moves. While they are often used interchangeably in everyday life, they have different scientific meanings.

Speed

Speed is the rate at which an object covers a distance, regardless of direction. It is a scalar quantity (magnitude only).

Speed = Distance / Time

Unit: meters per second (m/s)

Types of Speed

  • Uniform Speed: When an object covers equal distances in equal intervals of time.
  • Variable Speed: When the distance covered per unit time changes.
  • Average Speed: Total distance traveled divided by total time taken.
    • Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time
  • Instantaneous Speed: The speed of an object at a particular instant of time.

Velocity

Velocity is the rate of change of displacement of an object in a specified direction. It is a vector quantity (magnitude + direction).

Velocity = Displacement / Time

Unit: meters per second (m/s)

Types of Velocity

  • Uniform Velocity: When a body covers equal displacements in equal intervals of time in the same direction.
  • Variable Velocity: When either the magnitude or the direction (or both) change with time.
  • Average Velocity: Total displacement divided by total time taken.
    • Average Velocity = Total Displacement / Total Time
  • Instantaneous Velocity: Velocity of a body at a specific instant of time.

Difference Between Speed and Velocity

  • Speed is a scalar, Velocity is a vector.
  • Speed depends only on distance, Velocity depends on displacement.
  • Speed is always positive, Velocity can be positive, negative, or zero.

Example Problem

A car travels 120 km north in 2 hours.
(a) Find its average speed.
(b) Find its average velocity.

Distance = 120 km = 120,000 m Time = 2 h = 7200 s

(a) Average Speed = Distance / Time = 120,000 / 7200 = 16.67 m/s (b) Average Velocity = Displacement / Time = 120,000 / 7200 = 16.67 m/s north

Applications

  • In transportation (cars, planes, ships).
  • In sports (athlete performance tracking).
  • In physics experiments involving motion.
  • Navigation and GPS systems.

Conclusion

Speed and velocity are closely related but distinct concepts. While speed only tells us how fast, velocity tells us both how fast and in which direction.
