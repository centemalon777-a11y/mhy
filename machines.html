⚙️ Simple Machines

SS1 Physics – Comprehensive Notes

Introduction

A machine is a device that makes work easier by either multiplying force, changing its direction, or increasing speed. Simple machines are the most basic mechanical devices that apply a single force to do work.

Types of Simple Machines

  • Lever – A rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point (fulcrum).
    Examples: crowbar, scissors, seesaw.
  • Inclined Plane – A sloping surface used to raise or lower objects.
    Examples: ramps, slides.
  • Wheel and Axle – A larger wheel attached to a smaller axle.
    Examples: bicycle, steering wheel.
  • Pulley – A grooved wheel with a rope running through it, used to lift objects.
    Examples: flagpoles, cranes.
  • Screw – An inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder.
    Examples: bolts, jar lids.
  • Wedge – Two inclined planes joined together, used to split or cut objects.
    Examples: knife, axe.

Key Terms

  • Effort – The force applied to the machine.
  • Load – The resistance or object being moved.
  • Fulcrum – The pivot point of a lever.
  • Mechanical Advantage (M.A.) – Ratio of load to effort.
    M.A. = Load / Effort
  • Velocity Ratio (V.R.) – Ratio of distance moved by effort to distance moved by load.
    V.R. = Distance moved by effort ÷ Distance moved by load
  • Efficiency (η) – How well a machine converts input to useful output.
    Efficiency = (M.A. / V.R.) × 100%

Example

Example: A pulley system is used to lift a 200 N load with an effort of 50 N. Find the Mechanical Advantage.

M.A. = Load / Effort = 200 ÷ 50 = 4 → The pulley makes the work 4 times easier.

Applications of Simple Machines

  • Used in lifting heavy loads (cranes, pulleys).
  • Making cutting easier (knives, axes).
  • Transportation (wheel and axle in cars and bicycles).
  • Construction (ramps, screws, levers).

Conclusion

Simple machines are the foundation of all complex machines. They reduce human effort, save energy, and make tasks more efficient in everyday life.
