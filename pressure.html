Introduction

Pressure is defined as the force exerted per unit area of a surface. It explains how forces are distributed on surfaces in solids, liquids, and gases.

Formula for Pressure

Pressure (P) = Force (F) / Area (A)

Unit: Pascal (Pa) = N/m¬≤

Pressure in Solids

For solids, pressure depends on how force is applied and the area of contact.

  • Sharp objects exert high pressure because they have small areas.
  • Broad surfaces exert low pressure because of large areas.

Applications of Pressure

  • Hydraulic machines (car jacks, brakes, presses).
  • Syringes and pumps.
  • Air pressure in tires and balloons.
  • Drinking through a straw (suction principle).

P = F / A = 50 / 0.1 = 500 Pa

Conclusion

Pressure plays a vital role in understanding fluids, gases, and engineering designs. It explains many natural phenomena and technological applications.
