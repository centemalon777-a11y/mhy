Introduction

In physics, force is a fundamental concept that explains why objects move, stop, or change direction. Force can be described as a push or pull acting on an object, and it is a vector quantity because it has both magnitude and direction.

Definition of Force

A force is any interaction that, when unopposed, changes the motion of an object. It can cause an object to accelerate, decelerate, change direction, or change shape.

Unit of Force

The SI unit of force is the Newton (N). One Newton is defined as the force required to give a mass of 1 kg an acceleration of 1 m/sÂ².

Effects of Force

  • It can change the speed of a body (accelerate or decelerate it).
  • It can change the direction of motion of a body.
  • It can change the shape or size of an object (e.g., compressing or stretching).
  • It can stop a moving body.
